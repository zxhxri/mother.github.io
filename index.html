Tongue:

I want to question our human-centric role on this planet. Understanding that our existence has never been self-reliant. We feed off of others and they feed off of us. Fermentation processes in food production and human mentabolism are the cases which I based my research on. The disparity between humans with different biological, sexual, political, economic circumstances. I acknowledge language as a driving force in the way we perceive one another. Word-making is a political act. By understanding how language creates conventions, we can play with it to subvert them. Language is based on collective understandings of things, but they can also be created by those with more power. We need open access, free thought to generate alternative knowledge for a progressive life together. Dirty knowledge.

Fermentation } collective transformation ♺ of a substance is a process which inspired this thought process. An alien ritual within our homes. Microbes eat, we eat, then more microorganisms eat. The table is a stage for interactions. During the Graduation Show I will explore how language and taste collide through fermentation in a series of Tongue Acts.
