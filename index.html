Tongue:
I want to question our human-centric role on this planet. Understanding that our existence has never been self-reliant. We feed off of others and they feed of of us. The disparity between humans with different biological, sexual, political, economic circumstances. I acknowledge language as a driving force in the way we perceive one another. Word-making is a political act. By understanding how language creates conventions, we can play with it to subvert them. Language is based on collective understandings of things, but they can also be created by those with more power. We need open access, free thought to generate alternative knowledge for a progressive life together.
Fermentation} collective transformation of a substance is a process which inspired this narrative. An alien ritual in our homes. Microbes eat, we eat, then more microorganisms eat. The table is a stage for interactions. During the Graduation Show I will explore how language and taste collide in a series of Tongue Activities.
